Rule of Thirds

Sig Sauer

siqsauer.com

The Sig Sauer web site has an excellent example of the rule of third on their home page. As you can see here in the image the gun which is supposed to be the focal point is right on the subdevision that you would use for the rule of thirds.

Visual Hierarchy

Reddit

reddit.com

As you can see, this is a great example of visual hierarchy. Reddit has made the bigest thing on the screen "The conversation starts here". This lets you know as soon as you see the page a general idea of what you are getting into. Then the next bigest thing on the screen is the question "what is reddit about?". This is there to entice you to find out more. Next you have the title of the site "Reddit", Incase you forgot where you were. The menu button is also clearly visible. The fine details being the smallest.

White Space and Clean Design

Corsair

corsair.com

Corsair has done a really great job of using white space and clean design on their home page. They have included very little in the way of information on the page itself. There is an image of a CPU cooler with a title and a little slogan. Then they simple invite you to learn more. There is no need for more information here. They also list some other companies they want you to check out (most likely because they have part ownership.) They also don't even include their company name, just their logo. These makes looking at their page very easy to view.
